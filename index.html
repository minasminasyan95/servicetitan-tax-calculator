Getting started
Before entering items, query the invoice in your database to retrieve each line's amount, taxable status, and discountable status. Price modifiers (discount and fee items) are included and will appear at the bottom of the results — enter them in the Discount items section below, not as invoice items. 
SELECT
    ii.[Id],
    ii.[SkuName]        AS [Item Code],
    ii.[Description],
    ii.[Total],
    ii.[Taxable],
    ii.[AllowDiscounts] AS [Discountable],
    CASE ii.[SkuReference.SkuType]
        WHEN 0 THEN 'Service'
        WHEN 1 THEN 'Material'
        WHEN 2 THEN 'Equipment'
        WHEN 3 THEN 'Price Modifier'
    END AS [Item Type]
FROM [dbo].[InvoiceItem] ii
WHERE ii.[Invoice.Id] = @InvoiceID
  AND ii.[Active] = 1
ORDER BY ii.[SkuReference.SkuType];
Replace @InvoiceID with the target invoice ID before running.

How ServiceTitan Discount Items affect tax

Discount items in ServiceTitan are applied proportionally (prorated) across all discountable lines on the invoice — they do not simply subtract a flat amount.

Discountable pool
= sum of all discountable line amounts
Proration factor
= Total discount ÷ Discountable pool
Allocated discount
= Line amount × Proration factor
Adjusted line amount
= Line amount − Allocated discount
Only discountable lines receive an allocation. Non-discountable lines are unchanged. The adjusted amounts of taxable lines are summed to produce the new taxable total.

This can produce unexpected results in several scenarios:

Negative discountable pool If discountable lines include negative amounts (e.g., credits or negative service items) that bring the pool total below zero, the proration factor turns negative. A negative factor causes allocated discounts to be subtracted as negative numbers — which increases taxable line amounts rather than reducing them, resulting in a higher taxable total and more tax than expected.
Mixed taxable / non-taxable discountable lines Because the discount is spread proportionally across all discountable lines regardless of their taxability, the effective reduction on taxable lines may be larger or smaller than the face value of the discount — making tax outcomes difficult to predict.
Non-discountable taxable lines Any taxable line marked non-discountable is completely unaffected by the proration and retains its full original amount for tax purposes.
Our recommendation

We strongly recommend not using Discount Items on invoices where tax is being charged. Instead, apply discounts using negative-amount service items. Unlike discount items, negative service items can be marked as taxable, giving you direct and predictable control over how the discount affects the taxable total.
